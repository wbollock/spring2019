Blocks4All: Overcoming Accessibility Barriers to Blocks Programming for Children with Visual Impairments
Paper No. 69 
The real-world IT problem this paper attempts to solve is ease of access to programming for those that are
visually impaired. "Blocks-based" methods are currently used to teach programming to the visually impaired, but this study
examined five major issues this method currently faces.

Using Stakeholder Theory to Examine Drivers' Stake in Uber
Paper No.83
This paper examines Uber in the lense of "Stakeholder Theory." The overall problem this paper attempts to solve
is how people handle the "gig economy" by analzying Uber driver forum messages.

Watch Me Play: Does Social Facilitation Apply to Digital Games?
Paper No. 100
While not solving a tradational problem, this paper looks at the social facilitiation effect,
in which people change behaviors or experience different levels of performance in games when being observed.
This paper showed the in-game success was not influenced by "the presence of any social entity." It solved another layer of the problem
in the difference between physical actions and virtual ones.
